## "We're Not Comfortable Touching This One" — What Luxury Frame Owners Hear Most Often

You spend tens of thousands of NT dollars on a Cartier frame, have a friend bring back Chrome Hearts from Japan, or want to convert a Linda Farrow sunglasses frame you've had for years into prescription lenses — and after visiting a few optical shops, you keep hearing the same thing: "We're not comfortable disassembling this one."

It's not that the shop doesn't want the business — there's **genuine risk involved.** The structure, materials, and hinge design of luxury frames are completely different from mass-produced frames, and one wrong move during disassembly can mean damage you can't undo or pay to fix.

This article covers: what actually makes luxury frame fitting difficult, how to tell whether a shop is equipped to handle it, and whether a broken high-end frame can still be saved.

## What Makes Luxury Frame Fitting Difficult

### 1. Unusual Construction

Cartier's metal-craft locking mechanisms, Chrome Hearts' sterling silver engraving on thick acetate, Lindberg's screwless clasps, ic! berlin's spring-clip fittings — each has its own dedicated disassembly logic. Using standard techniques on these can scratch or, worse, break them.

### 2. Little Room for Error in Lens Cutting

Luxury frames often have non-standard groove depths and curvatures, giving lens cutting far less tolerance than mass-produced frames. Cut too large and the lens won't fit; cut too small and it loosens and pops out — either way, the damage is irreversible.

### 3. Optics and Aesthetics Both Have to Work

Put a high-prescription lens into a large frame, and the edge thickness will be very apparent. Fitting a luxury frame means solving for **optical center alignment** and **lens thickness control** (refractive index selection, frame-shape recommendations) at the same time — otherwise you end up with a beautiful frame and a pair of glasses that feels like a shame to wear.

## Can a Broken Lindberg or ic! berlin Frame Still Be Saved?

Screwless construction is part of what makes high-end frames appealing — and it's also what makes them harder to repair:

- **Lindberg:** screwless pure titanium clasps — loose temple arms or deformed nose pads require specialized techniques to reposition
- **ic! berlin:** spring-clip construction — fatigued or deformed spring clips can be adjusted and repaired
- **Götti:** precision titanium craftsmanship — repositioning a misaligned frame requires experience with the material's spring-back properties
