## What Kind of Brand Is HOYA?

HOYA was founded in 1941 and is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan. It started out manufacturing optical glass and grew into one of the largest eyeglass lens manufacturers in the world, with businesses spanning eyeglass lenses, medical devices, and semiconductor optical components.

In the lens business, HOYA's core strength lies in its **vertically integrated manufacturing** — from raw optical glass, through lens design, to final coating, nearly the entire process is handled in-house, ensuring consistent optical quality across every lens.

## HOYA's Main Lens Series

### Single-Vision Lenses: The Nulux Series

Nulux is HOYA's flagship single-vision lens series, using an **aspheric design** that, compared to traditional spherical lenses, effectively reduces peripheral aberration for a clearer field of view and a thinner, lighter lens.

Nulux EP (Enhanced Performance) further optimizes the design around the eye's natural rotation, making image quality more stable as your gaze shifts off-center — an especially noticeable benefit for people with high myopia.

**Best for:** anyone needing simple myopia, hyperopia, or astigmatism correction who wants a thin, clear lens.

---

### Digital-Strain Relief Lenses: Sync III

For people who spend long hours on their phone or computer, near-distance use dominates the visual routine. Sync III is HOYA's functional lens designed for this usage pattern, building a subtle **near-support design** into the lower part of the lens. It looks identical to a regular single-vision lens and needs no adaptation to a progressive zone; whether it suits you depends on your individual visual needs and should be assessed through an eye exam.

This isn't a reading lens, and it isn't a progressive multifocal. It has virtually no effect on appearance, making it suited to people aged 20–40 who use screens more than 6 hours a day but don't yet need multifocal correction.

**Best for:** office workers and students who spend long hours on digital devices and notice their eyes feel sore and tired after work.

---

### Progressive Multifocal Lenses: The iD Series

HOYA's iD series is its flagship progressive multifocal lens line, using a **double-surface design** — the optical design for the distance, intermediate, and near zones is distributed across both the front and back surfaces of the lens. Compared to traditional single-surface progressive designs, this provides a wider field of clear vision and a shorter adaptation period.

The iD series comes in several versions:
- **iD EaZi:** an entry-level progressive, suited to people trying progressive lenses for the first time
- **iD Space:** optimized for indoor environments, suited to people who spend long hours in an office
- **iD MySelf:** a fully personalized version, calculated based on the wearer's eye position, habitual head posture, and lifestyle

**Best for:** presbyopic wearers, older adults who need clear vision at distance, intermediate, and near ranges simultaneously, and anyone who wants to reduce how often they switch glasses.

---

### Defocus Lens Design: MiYOSMART

MiYOSMART is a defocus lens design for children, developed by HOYA in partnership with The Hong Kong Polytechnic University, using **DIMS technology** (Defocus Incorporated Multiple Segments) — the lens surface carries multiple micro-defocus segments that shift the focal point of part of the incoming light, providing a different visual stimulus.

This is a functional lens that needs to be assessed and fitted by a licensed optometrist, suited to school-age children with tracking needs, and recommended alongside regular vision assessments.

**Best for:** school-age children 6 and older with a need for regular vision tracking, chosen jointly by parents and an optometrist.

---

### Coating Technology: Hi-Vision LongLife

HOYA's coating lineup is represented by Hi-Vision, offering anti-reflective, scratch-resistant, water-repellent, and dust-repellent properties, with some higher-tier versions adding **blue-light filtering**.

Coating quality directly affects a lens's lifespan and visual comfort — an easily overlooked but genuinely important part of choosing a lens.

## The Logic Behind Choosing a HOYA Lens

With this many series under one brand, not everyone needs the top-tier version. The logic for choosing a lens should start from **your actual visual needs**:

- How many hours a day do you spend looking at a screen?
- Do you need clear vision at distance, intermediate, and near simultaneously?
- How strong is your prescription? Does lens thickness matter to you?
- How quickly is your child's vision changing, and what tracking frequency do they need?

The answers to these questions determine which series is right for you — not whichever one an ad tells you is best.
